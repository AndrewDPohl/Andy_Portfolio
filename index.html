About me

Passion Breeds Creativity

Born out of the desire to have a career centered around creativity and having more freedom, I engulfed myself in the world of web development. Through my own learning, and a stint at General Assembly in San Francisco, I now have the skills needed to design and produce web applications and websites. Outside of web development, I am an active musician, operate a small record label called Sell The Heart Records, and am a contributing writer for SHOWBAMS and Noise Pop.

My Projects

whutzOpen?

Ever been in a new city and are unsure what food venues might be open? whutzOpen? is the solution for you. This was my first application ever, built during my coursework at General Assembly. Technologies used: Node.js | Express.js | Passport.js | PostgreSQL | MapBox API | Foursquare API

Visit Site

The Senator Accountability Project

A collaborative project, The Senator Accountability Project aims to provide up-to-date and relevant data on the current members of the United States Senate, to help keep voters informed when they go to cast their vote. My second web application, also complete during my coursework at General Assembly, along with my classmates Samuel F. Cabral and Sandra Stark. Technologies used: Ruby on Rails | PostgreSQL | New York Times API | GovTrack.us API | Chart.js

Visit Site

GymPrtnr

Born out of the desire to find other people in my local area that shared a similar interest in working out, GYMPRTNR connects you based on the sports of your choice. My third project as part of my coursework at General Assembly. Technologies used: Angular.js | Rails | OmniAuth | PostgreSQL
